Usage[edit]

{{speedydelete}} marks an item for speedy deletion. You always have to give a reason for a valid speedydelete.

  • It should meet the requirements for speedy deletion.
  • Usually placed at the top of the item.

Multilingual templates[edit]

Should such templates be multilingual? For example:

This page is a candidate for speedy deletion. If you disagree with its speedy deletion, please explain this on its talk page.
Esta página encontra-se vazia por ter sido criada com lixo, que apagámos. Se sabe alguma coisa sobre o assunto, por favor, crie um esboço em substituição desta nota.
Diese Seite wurde nach den Regeln des Schnelllöschverfahrens zum sofortigen Löschen vorgeschlagen. Einsprüche bitte auf die Diskussionsseite schreiben.
このページは即時削除の対象となっており、間もなく削除される予定です。もしこのページの削除に同意できなければ、記事を適切な内容に改めた上で 即時削除 を取り除いてください。

Or would this end up with too many languages showing on the same page? Should the language of the template match the language in the page being deleted? For example, should someone who speaks Portuguese check pages with {{lixo}} on them, and people who speak English only check ones with {{delete}} on them? This makes some sense since people not speaking Portuguese would not know whether the page really was patent nonsense. Angela 16:17, 10 Sep 2004 (UTC)

Hide the note if `category` is set[edit]

{{Edit request}} This template includes a note instructing editors to use a more specific deletion template. However, {{Speedydelete}} is used as the base for some more-specific templates (such as {{Fair use}} and {{Standard YouTube License}}; see also Invalid license tags), and in those cases, this note doesn't make sense. The one thing all those templates have in common is that they set the category parameter to override the default category of Other speedy deletions. I think {{Speedydelete/layout}} should be updated to hide the note if the category parameter is set. –IagoQnsi (talk) 22:33, 29 June 2020 (UTC)Reply[reply]

Please be specific on what you want changed. 1989 (talk) 19:59, 1 August 2021 (UTC)Reply[reply]

Add NOINDEX[edit]

{{Edit request}} To the end of the first line of the template, add:

{{NOINDEX}}

This will stop an image from being spidered and indexed by search engines in the time between it being flagged and either deleted or unflagged, the same as happens currently on Wikipedia when an article is flagged for speedy deletion.

I suggested it at Commons:Village_pump/Proposals/Archive/2022/03#Add_noindex_to_speedy_deletion_templates last month, the post just got two comments that it was "reasonable", the first also asking if Google would know to remove such images (I believe it would), the second descending into free association. --Lord Belbury (talk) 10:36, 24 April 2022 (UTC)Reply[reply]

 Done in Special:Diff/652441060. --TKsdik8900 (talk) 08:45, 28 April 2022 (UTC)Reply[reply]

Does anyone know why that is? --Lord Belbury (talk) 08:53, 28 April 2022 (UTC)Reply[reply]

This workshop is part of the Graphics Lab, a project aimed at picture retouching to improve the graphical content of the Wikimedia projects. More information about the lab can be found on its main page and requests pages (Illustrations ; Photographs ; Maps ; Video and Sound). To ask questions or make a suggestions, see the talk page of the graphic lab page.

This specific page is the requests page for the Illustration Workshop. Anyone can make a request for an illustration to be created or improved. The standard format for making a request is shown below, along with general advice, and should be followed.

  • Cross pattée

  • Coat of arms of Oliwa

Article(s): w:Flag of Gdańsk, en:Free City of Danzig (Napoleonic)

Request
Please make some historical flags of the city of Gdansk.
  1. Burgee of Danzig-Zoppoter Yacht-Club Gode Wind
  2. Free City of Danzig (1807–1814). According to this website, in Napoleon's time a flag with a pattée cross and a crown of leaves were introduced. Since the exact appearance of the crown is unknown, use a crown from the interwar period (in 1920 they defined this pattern as the restoration of the symbol of the first free city).
  3. Free City of Danzig flag proposal (1807). Same symbols as above, but a blue background like the French flag.
  4. Flag of Gdańsk (variant), Contains elements from the 90s flag but shifted towards the mast as on the current flag. It is still in use, for example, I saw it in the photo from the anniversary celebration of the Westerplatte attack as if from a street lamp. Only the black contours of the cross should be slightly wider.
  5. Flag of Oliwa (1924-1926). Do it with a slightly brighter green and without lines and gray trapezoid present in the coat of arms I provided.
  6. House flag of Gdansk Shipyard

At small display sizes, there is a noticeable moiré aliasing, most noticeable at hues which are at angles of 90 degrees. --–LaundryPizza03 (dc̄) 10:56, 11 October 2022 (UTC)Reply[reply]
Graphist opinion(s)
@LaundryPizza03:
and...
what is your request?
En rouge (talk) 22:36, 11 October 2022 (UTC)Reply[reply]
  • I recommend using a PNG (download and reupload the 2048px version to Commons). The existing SVG standard does not allow a "conical" / radial gradient, which means that the trick employed in this SVG (dividing the circle into 360 "pizza slice" shapes) is necessary to ensure an accurate presentation of the color space, yet with the side effect of that alias rendering. Maybe someone else in this lab can think of a more ingenious hack to improve the situation? --痛 (talk) 23:38, 11 October 2022 (UTC)Reply[reply]

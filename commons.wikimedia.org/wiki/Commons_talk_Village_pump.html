Christie's -copyright[edit]

Sorry; I have very little knowledge about copyright; I see that there are a lot of pictures from Christie's https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:Images_from_Christie%27s

But is it possible to upload images from here? Huldra (talk) 23:13, 6 December 2021 (UTC)Reply[reply]

The general idea is that a photo of a 3D object (such as a stela) is a creative process, and therefore for such photos both the object and a photo must be out of copyright. For 2D object such as paintings the process is not creative, and therefore if a painting is out of copyright its photo of any provenance can be uploaded on Commons. I am not so sure about the books (I hope someone will give an explanation), butreliefs and jewelry unfortunately can not be uploaded on Commons unless the photo is old or a photographer has released it under a free license.--Ymblanter (talk) 15:22, 7 December 2021 (UTC)Reply[reply]
Thanks, User:Ymblanter; sigh, I suspected that was the case, thanks for confirming it, cheers, Huldra (talk) 20:51, 7 December 2021 (UTC)Reply[reply]
